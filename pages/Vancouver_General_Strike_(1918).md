The **Vancouver General Strike of 1918** was the
"<a   href="List_of_General_Strikes.md" class="wikilink"
title="first general strike">"first general strike</a> in
"<a   href="Canada.md" class="wikilink" title="Canadian">"Canadian</a> history
and took place on the
"<a   href="Timeline_of_Anarchism_in_North_America.md" class="wikilink"
title="2nd of August">"2nd of August</a>
"<a   href="Revolutions_of_1916_-_1923.md" class="wikilink"
title="1918">"1918</a>, it lasted only one day. It was part of a broader
strike wave in Canada and the USA.

## Background

The strike had several causes, including:

- Anger at conscription
- Increased cost of living and stagnant wages caused by
  "<a   href="World_War_I.md" class="wikilink" title="World War I">"World War
  I</a>
- Banning of strikes and socialist media
- The "<a   href="October_Revolution_(Russia).md" class="wikilink"
  title="October Revolution">"October Revolution</a> in Russia
- The killing of trade union and anti-war activist Albert "Ginger"
  Goodwin by a private police officer

## Events

The strike occurred after the killing of union activist Albert Goodwin,
and soldiers immediately tried to repress the strike, claiming it to be
a "<a   href="Bolsheviks.md" class="wikilink" title="Bolshevik">"Bolshevik</a>
conspiracy. Three hundred soldiers ransacked union officers and forced a
union secretary to kiss the Union Jack (the flag of the
"<a   href="United_Kingdom.md" class="wikilink" title="UK">"UK</a>). Although
most union leaders resigned, all were reelected in the ensuing union
elections.
