The **Zapatista Army of National Liberation** (Spanish: *Ejército
Zapatista de Liberación Nacional*, **EZLN**), often referred to as the
**Zapatistas** are a <a href="Neo-Zapatismo" class="wikilink"
title="Neo-Zapatist">Neo-Zapatist</a> rebel group in
<a href="Mexico" class="wikilink" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>, that has
been responsible for the <a href="Zapatista_Revolution" class="wikilink"
title="Zapatista Revolution">Zapatista Revolution</a> and the creation
of the
<a href="Rebel_Zapatista_Autonomous_Municipalities" class="wikilink"
title="Rebel Zapatista Autonomous Municipalities">Rebel Zapatista
Autonomous Municipalities</a>. They are the main participants in the
<a href="Chiapas_Conflict" class="wikilink"
title="Chiapas Conflict">Chiapas Conflict</a>. Mainly made up of rural
indigenous people, there are supporters in Mexican towns and cities as
well as internationally.

## History

### Background

The EZLN is a product of 500 years of indigenous resistance to
colonialism, capitalism and ecocide. Drawing inspirations from the long
history of indigenous
<a href="commons" class="wikilink" title="commons">commons</a> and armed
uprisings to the Mexican government (notably led by
<a href="Emiliano_Zapata" class="wikilink"
title="Emiliano Zapata">Emiliano Zapata</a> of the
<a href="Mexican_Revolution" class="wikilink"
title="Mexican Revolution">Mexican Revolution</a>) as well as
<a href="Marxism" class="wikilink" title="Marxist">Marxist</a> and
<a href="Anarchism" class="wikilink" title="anarchist">anarchist</a>
theory.

In the aftermath of the <a href="Tlatelolco_Massacre" class="wikilink"
title="Tlatelolco Massacre">Tlatelolco Massacre</a>, much of Mexico's
left concluded that peaceful, nonviolent protests were doomed to fail.
Leftist student brothers organised to form the FLN (National Liberation
Front), a <a href="Marxist-Leninism" class="wikilink"
title="Marxist-Leninist">Marxist-Leninist</a> group that operated in
secrecy and developed covert networks and safehouses, After being
destroyed by police raids, they attempted to recruit more from extremely
poor indigenous peasants. Setting up safe houses in towns in Chiapas and
secret training camps in the Jungle and contacting a network of
solidarity organisations like Maoist groups, cooperative organisers,
churchs and community organisations.
