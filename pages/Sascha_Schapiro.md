**Alexander "Sascha" Schapiro** (1890 - 1942) was an
<a href="Anarchism.md" class="wikilink" title="anarchist">anarchist</a> and
soldier in the <a href="Russian_Revolution.md" class="wikilink"
title="Russian Revolution">Russian Revolution</a> and
<a href="Spanish_Civil_War.md" class="wikilink"
title="Spanish Civil War">Spanish Civil War</a>.

## Life

### Childhood

Born into a family of Hasidic Jews in the border town of Novozybkov. He
began to identify more with the working class and peasantry of Russia
than his own wealthy family. Leaving the town at 14 to join an
<a href="Insurrectionary_Anarchism.md" class="wikilink"
title="insurrectionist anarchist">insurrectionist anarchist</a> group
similar to the
<a href="Chernoe_Znamia.md" class="wikilink" title="Chernoe Znamia">Chernoe
Znamia</a>. The group was repressed by police in 1905, after the
<a href="Russian_Revolution_(1905).md" class="wikilink"
title="failed Russian Revolution">failed Russian Revolution</a> and an
attempt to assassinate <a href="Tsar_Nicholas_II.md" class="wikilink"
title="Tsar Nicholas II">Tsar Nicholas II</a>.

### Arrest

The rest of the group was executed, but Sascha was sentenced to life in
prison on account of his youth and he was placed in a Moscow dungeon.
However, the influence of a friend managed to secure a position in the
nicer Yaroslavl prison. His left arm was shot while trying to escape,
resulting in its amputation.After an attempted suicide, he spent the
year 1914 in solitary confinement.

### Release and Russian Revolution

Sascha was released from prison in 1917, with the collapse of the
Czarist regime and new
<a href="Provisional_Government_(Russian_Revolution).md" class="wikilink"
title="Provisional Government">Provisional Government</a> led by
<a href="Alexander_Kerensky.md" class="wikilink"
title="Alexander Kerensky">Alexander Kerensky</a>. Hailed as a hero
across the country, he became an outspoken critic of the new regime.
Befriending the anarchist revolutionaries
<a href="Lev_Chernyi.md" class="wikilink" title="Lev Chernyi">Lev
Chernyi</a> and <a href="Maria_Nikiforova.md" class="wikilink"
title="Maria Nikiforova">Maria Nikiforova</a>, and joining the
<a href="Revolutionary_Insurrectionary_Army_of_Ukraine.md" class="wikilink"
title="Black Army">Black Army</a>. He married a woman named Rachel, and
had a son, Dodek. However, repression of the anarchists by the
<a href="Bolsheviks.md" class="wikilink" title="Bolsheviks">Bolsheviks</a>
led him to flee to Minsk, where he met
<a href="Alexander_Berkman.md" class="wikilink"
title="Alexander Berkman">Alexander Berkman</a>. He escaped Russia
through the Polish border in 1921, using forged papers.

### Life in Europe

By 1922, Schapiro had reached Berlin, where he remained except for a few
travels to Paris and Belgium until 1924. He befriended other anarchists
there, such as <a href="Francisco_Ascaso.md" class="wikilink"
title="Francisco Ascaso">Francisco Ascaso</a>,
<a href="Buenaventura_Durruti.md" class="wikilink"
title="Buenaventura Durruti">Buenaventura Durruti</a>,
<a href="Francesco_Ghezzi.md" class="wikilink"
title="Francesco Ghezzi">Francesco Ghezzi</a> and
<a href="Theodor_Plievier.md" class="wikilink"
title="Theodor Plievier">Theodor Plievier</a>. He remained in contact
with Nestor Makhno and members of the
<a href="Dielo_Truda.md" class="wikilink" title="Dielo Truda">Dielo
Truda</a>. Along with <a href="Sébastien_Faure.md" class="wikilink"
title="Sébastien Faure">Sébastien Faure</a>,
<a href="Ugo_Fedeli.md" class="wikilink" title="Ugo Fedeli">Ugo Fedeli</a>
and <a href="Maksymilian_Horwitz.md" class="wikilink"
title="Henryk Walecki">Henryk Walecki</a> he founded the Paris-section
of the International Works of Anarchist Editions, run by
<a href="Severin_Ferandel.md" class="wikilink"
title="Severin Ferandel">Severin Ferandel</a>. He met
<a href="Hanka_Grothendieck.md" class="wikilink"
title="Hanka Grothendieck">Hanka Grothendieck</a> while working as a
street photographer, and they had a son,
<a href="Alexander_Grothendieck.md" class="wikilink"
title="Alexander">Alexander</a>.

### Spanish Civil War

Forced to flee
<a href="Nazi_Germany.md" class="wikilink" title="Germany">Germany</a>
after the rise to power of
<a href="Adolf_Hitler.md" class="wikilink" title="Adolf Hitler">Adolf
Hitler</a>, and intent on fighting in the coming
<a href="Spanish_Civil_War.md" class="wikilink"
title="Spanish Civil War">Spanish Civil War</a>, the couple sent
Alexander to live with the Heydorns, a middle-class family with
anarchist sympathies, in 1933. He worked as a soldier in
<a href="Spain.md" class="wikilink" title="Spain">Spain</a>, fighting until
the bitter end of the war, where he fled to France, being interned at
<a href="Camp_Vernet.md" class="wikilink" title="Camp Vernet">Camp
Vernet</a>.

### Nazi Germany

Sascha lived in Nazi-Occupied Paris, active in the underground anarchist
movement, but was soon arrested and deported to
<a href="Auschwitz.md" class="wikilink" title="Auschwitz">Auschwitz</a>
concentration camp in 1942, where he was killed.[^1]
