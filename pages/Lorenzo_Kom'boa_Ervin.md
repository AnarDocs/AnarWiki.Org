**Lorenzo Kom'boa Ervin**(1947) is an
<a href="Anarchism.md" class="wikilink" title="anarchist">anarchist</a> and
<a href="Autonomism.md" class="wikilink" title="autonomist">autonomist</a>
activist and writer, being a theorist for
<a href="Black_Anarchism.md" class="wikilink" title="Black Anarchism">Black
Anarchism</a>.

## Life

### Early Activism

When he was 12, Ervin joined the
<a href="NAACP.md" class="wikilink" title="NAACP">NAACP</a> youth group and
participated in the sit-in protests that helped end
<a href="Jim_Crow.md" class="wikilink" title="racial segregation">racial
segregation</a> in Chattanooga. He was drafted during the
<a href="Vietnam_War.md" class="wikilink" title="Vietnam War">Vietnam
War</a> and served in the army for two years, where he became an
<a href="Peace_Movements.md" class="wikilink"
title="anti-war activist">anti-war activist</a>. In 1967 he joined the
<a href="SNCC.md" class="wikilink"
title="Student Nonviolent Coordinating Committee">Student Nonviolent
Coordinating Committee</a> and, a short time later, the
<a href="Black_Panther_Party.md" class="wikilink"
title="Black Panther Party">Black Panther Party</a>.
