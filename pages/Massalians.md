Formed in 4th-century CE Syria, the Massalians were a gnostic Christian
heresy which believed that a period of strict asceticism enabled
practitioners to reach a condition of such purity that they subsequently
did not have to worry about sin and could indulge themselves sexually as
they wished. The Massalians had female and male leaders. They would
