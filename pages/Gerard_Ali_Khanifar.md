**Gerard Ali Khanifar** (10 August 1950 - 21 February 1997) was a French
<a href="anarchism.md" class="wikilink" title="anarchist">anarchist</a> and
worker at Michelin.

## Biography

Born of an Arabic and North African background in France, he was bullied
in school. He worked for the Michelin company and became an anarchist in
1968 during the May 68 protests in France.

He was imprisoned for his solidarity with Spanish and Portuguese
anarchists who were engaged in struggle against the dictators Franco and
Salazar. He also assisted with striking truckrivers and casual theatre
workers in France, as well as organising among the unemployed. He also
supported the British miners strike in 1984-85. He was the only objector
to the French national census to appear in court.

He was blacklisted by employers and obtained a diploma in 1993, having
studied the work of
<a href="Mikhail_Bakunin.md" class="wikilink" title="Bakunin">Bakunin</a>.
