**Wilstar Choongo** (1964 - 1999) was a
"<a   href="List_of_Libertarian_Socialists.md" class="wikilink"
title="librarian">"librarian</a> and
"<a   href="Anarchism.md" class="wikilink" title="anarchist">"anarchist</a> who
organised the Anarchist & Workers' Solidarity Movement (AWSM) of
"<a   href="Zambia.md" class="wikilink" title="Zambia">"Zambia</a>. Born to
"<a   href="Peasants.md" class="wikilink" title="peasant farmers">"peasant
farmers</a> in rural Zambia, he joined worked as a
"<a   href="University.md" class="wikilink"
title="university librarian">"university librarian</a> in Lusaka, the
largest city in Zambia.

He was a self-taught political organiser and managed to get a pay rise
for university staff at the library he worked at in 1996, also
establishing links with the
"<a   href="Workers&#39;_Solidarity_Federation_(South_Africa).md"
class="wikilink" title="Workers&#39; Solidarity Federation">"Workers'
Solidarity Federation</a> (WSF) in
"<a   href="South_Africa.md" class="wikilink" title="South Africa">"South
Africa</a>. He also collected a lot of anarchist literature and
described as "a friendly, shambling man with a ready grin.
"<a   href="Michael_Schmidt.md" class="wikilink" title="I">"I</a> well recall
sitting on the bed in his cramped apartment eating a meal of eggs, bread
and oranges and engaging in fervent discussions on anarchist
strategies."

He often gave talks about anarchism,
"<a   href="Trade_Union.md" class="wikilink" title="trade unionism">"trade
unionism</a> and government corruption. But he died of malaria brought
about by menigitis at age 35, leaving behind a wife and three young
children. Unfortuantely, the Anarchist & Workers' Solidarity Movement
collapsed after his death.[^1]
