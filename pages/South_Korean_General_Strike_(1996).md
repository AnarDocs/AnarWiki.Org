The **South Korean General Strike of 1996-1997** was a
<a href="List_of_General_Strikes" class="wikilink"
title="general strike">general strike</a> that occurred in
<a href="South_Korea" class="wikilink" title="South Korea">South
Korea</a> in
<a href="Timeline_of_Anarchism_in_Eastern_Asia" class="wikilink"
title="1996 and 1997">1996 and 1997</a> as trade unionists fought
against a new law that would have legalised the firing of workers,
increased the workweek by 12 hours, legalise scab labor and ban strike
funds.

## Results

The general strike eventually led to the law being blocked, however, 11
months later it became legal to fire workers as part of a wave of
reforms triggered by the
<a href="Asian_Financial_Crisis_(1997)" class="wikilink"
title="Asian Financial Crisis of 1997">Asian Financial Crisis of
1997</a>.
