**Iris Pavon** or **Alejandrina Serrat** (1906-1951) was an Argentine
anarchist and writer.

## Life

Pavón was born in 1906 in the small pampas town of
"<a   href="Loberia.md" class="wikilink" title="Loberia">"Loberia</a>.[^1] She
later relocated to "<a   href="Deán_Funes,_Cordoba.md" class="wikilink"
title="Deán Funes, Cordoba">"Deán Funes, Cordoba</a> and then to
"<a   href="Cruz_del_Eje.md" class="wikilink" title="Cruz del Eje">"Cruz del
Eje</a>, where she came to maturity as a writer.

Anarchist in her beliefs,[^2] Pavón was very vocal on issues of social
justice. She took up the pen on behalf of the
"<a   href="Bragado.md" class="wikilink" title="Bragado">"Bragado</a> case of
1931 and the "<a   href="Sacco-Vanzetti.md" class="wikilink"
title="Sacco-Vanzetti">"Sacco-Vanzetti</a> case in the US.[^3] She
contributed to radical journals such as
*"<a   href="Reconstruir.md" class="wikilink"
title="Reconstruir">"Reconstruir</a>*. She was imprisoned in 1944
alongside her partner "<a   href="Marcos_Dukelsky.md" class="wikilink"
title="Marcos Dukelsky">"Marcos Dukelsky</a>.[^4]

Among her poems is "Huesos", dedicated to a worker who died during the
construction of the
"<a   href="Cruz_del_Eje.md" class="wikilink" title="Cruz del Eje">"Cruz del
Eje</a> dam. A collection of her texts, from the 1920s to the 1940s, was
published two years after her death under the title *Pasión de
Justicia*.[^5] It was reprinted in 2019.

She died in September 1951.[^6][^7]
