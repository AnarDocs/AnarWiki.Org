The **Workers' Liberation Group** was an
"<a   href="Anarcho-Communism.md" class="wikilink"
title="anarcho-communist">anarcho-communist</a> group active in
"<a   href="Iraq.md" class="wikilink" title="Iraq">Iraq</a> from
"<a   href="Revolutions_of_1967_-_1975.md" class="wikilink"
title="1973">1973</a> to
"<a   href="Timeline_of_Anarchism_in_Western_Asia.md" class="wikilink"
title="1978">1978</a> and
"<a   href="Iran.md" class="wikilink" title="Iran">Iran</a> from 1978 to 1979.
It had around 300 members.

## History

The group was founded after its members split from the
"<a   href="Iraqi_Communist_Party.md" class="wikilink"
title="Iraqi Communist Party">Iraqi Communist Party</a> for its alliance
with the "<a   href="Ba&#39;athist_Iraq.md" class="wikilink"
title="ruling government">ruling government</a> and 'waged a bitter
campaign' (wording in source is unclear, likely refers to
"<a   href="Guerilla_Warfare.md" class="wikilink"
title="guerilla warfare">guerilla warfare</a>) against the secret
police. In 1978 the entire group illegally crossed to Iran to help the
anarchist group
"<a   href="The_Scream_of_The_People_(Iran).md" class="wikilink"
title="The Scream of The People">The Scream of The People</a> to help
organise "<a   href="Workers&#39;_Council.md" class="wikilink"
title="workers&#39; councils">workers' councils</a> and
"<a   href="Democratic_Assembly.md" class="wikilink"
title="popular assemblies">popular assemblies</a> known as
"<a   href="Shoras_(Iran).md" class="wikilink" title="Shoras">Shoras</a> in
the "<a   href="Iranian_Revolution.md" class="wikilink"
title="Iranian Revolution">Iranian Revolution</a>.
