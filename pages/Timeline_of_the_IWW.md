A timeline of the
"<a   href="Industrial_Workers_of_the_World.md" class="wikilink"
title="Industrial Workers of the World">"Industrial Workers of the
World</a> from its formation in 1905 to the present day.

## 1900s

- 1905: The IWW is founded at a socialist congress in Chicago, USA by
  200
  "<a   href="Anarchism.md" class="wikilink" title="anarchist">"anarchist</a>,
  "<a   href="socialism.md" class="wikilink" title="socialist">"socialist</a>
  and
  "<a   href="communism.md" class="wikilink" title="communist">"communist</a>
  "<a   href="activism.md" class="wikilink" title="activist">"activists</a>.
  "<a   href="Big_Bill_Haywood.md" class="wikilink" title="Big Bill Haywood">"Big
  Bill Haywood</a>,
  "<a   href="James_Connolly.md" class="wikilink" title="James Connolly">"James
  Connolly</a>,
  "<a   href="Daniel_De_Leon.md" class="wikilink" title="Daniel De Leon">"Daniel
  De Leon</a>,
  "<a   href="Eugene_V._Debs.md" class="wikilink" title="Eugene V. Debs">"Eugene
  V. Debs</a>,
  "<a   href="Thomas_Hagerty.md" class="wikilink" title="Thomas Hagerty">"Thomas
  Hagerty</a>,
  "<a   href="Lucy_Parsons.md" class="wikilink" title="Lucy Parsons">"Lucy
  Parsons</a>,
  "<a   href="Mother_Jones.md" class="wikilink" title="Mother Jones">"Mother
  Jones</a>,
  "<a   href="Frank_Bohn.md" class="wikilink" title="Frank Bohn">"Frank Bohn</a>,
  "<a   href="William_Trautmann.md" class="wikilink"
  title="William Trautmann">"William Trautmann</a>,
  "<a   href="Vincent_Saint_John.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Vincent Saint John">"Vincent Saint John</a>,
  "<a   href="Ralph_Chaplin.md" class="wikilink" title="Ralph Chaplin">"Ralph
  Chaplin</a>, and many others.
- 1906: IWW affiliated miners with the
  "<a   href="Western_Federation_of_Miners.md" class="wikilink"
  title="WFM">"WFM</a> work together to
  "<a   href="1906-1907_Goldfield_Strike.md" class="wikilink"
  title="strike in Goldfield">"strike in Goldfield</a>, Nevada,
  "<a   href="United_States_of_America.md" class="wikilink" title="USA">"USA</a>
  over "<a   href="Wage_Labour.md" class="wikilink" title="wages">"wages</a>,
  resulting in a conflict between the unions and US soldiers.
- 1908: The IWW begins organizing workers' councils with meat industry
  workers in Queensland, Australia.
- 1909: The IWW battles police for wage increases during the
  "<a   href="McKees_Rocks_Strike_(1909).md" class="wikilink"
  title="McKees Rocks Strike">"McKees Rocks Strike</a>.

### 1910s

- 1910: The IWW is first organized in South Africa.
- 1912: The IWW organizes the
  "<a   href="Bread_and_Roses_Strike_(1912).md" class="wikilink"
  title="Bread and Roses Strike">"Bread and Roses Strike</a>, fighting
  police as they try and achieve a 54-hour workweek and 15% wage
  increase for immigrant workers.
- 1912: The IWW organizes the
  "<a   href="New_York_City_Waiters’_Strike_(1912).md" class="wikilink"
  title="first hospitality workers strike">"first hospitality workers
  strike</a>, with 6000 hospitality workers in New York City, USA going
  out on strike, resulting in 117,000 lost working days for the city.
- 1913: The IWW helps organize the unsuccessful
  "<a   href="Paterson_Silk_Strike.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Paterson Silk Strike">"Paterson Silk Strike</a>.
- 1915: The IWW helps form the
  "<a   href="Agricultural_Workers&#39;_Organization_(IWW).md" class="wikilink"
  title="Agricultural Workers&#39; Organization">"Agricultural Workers'
  Organization</a> (AWO) to organize farmers.
- 1915: The IWW helps organize
  "<a   href="Bayonne_Refinery_Strikes_(1915–1916).md" class="wikilink"
  title="two strikes">"two strikes</a> at the Bayonne Refinery in New
  Jersey, USA.
- 1917: The IWW peaks in membership in August, with 150,000 members.
- 1917: The US government illegally
  "<a   href="Bisbee_Deportation.md" class="wikilink"
  title="kidnaps and deports">"kidnaps and deports</a> 1,300 striking
  miners, many of whom are affiliated with the IWW.
- 1917: The IWW is blamed for the anti-conscription
  "<a   href="Green_Corn_Rebellion.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Green Corn Rebellion">"Green Corn Rebellion</a> despite the IWW
  having no role in the incident.
- 1917: The police secretly agree to allow white supremacists in Tulsa,
  Oklahoma, USA to kidnap IWW members.
- 1919: IWW workers help organize the
  "<a   href="Seattle_Uprising_(1919).md" class="wikilink"
  title="Seattle Uprising">"Seattle Uprising</a>.
- 1919: IWW-affiliated timber workers are murdered during the
  "<a   href="Centralia_Tragedy.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Centralia Tragedy">"Centralia Tragedy</a>.
- 1919: The "<a   href="Battle_of_Brewery_Gulch.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Battle of Brewery Gulch">"Battle of Brewery Gulch</a> occurs as
  the IWW assists many of the black veterans in the battle.

### 1920s

- 1920: IWW-affiliated miners are
  "<a   href="Anaconda_Road_Massacre.md" class="wikilink"
  title="murdered during a strike">"murdered during a strike</a> at
  Anaconda Road.
- 1923: The IWW battles police and the
  "<a   href="Ku_Klux_Klan.md" class="wikilink" title="Ku Klux Klan">"Ku Klux
  Klan</a> during the
  "<a   href="Liberty_Hill_Strike_(1923).md" class="wikilink"
  title="Liberty Hill Strike">"Liberty Hill Strike</a>

### 1930s

- 1933: The "<a   href="Congdon_Orchards_Battle_(1933).md" class="wikilink"
  title="Congdon Orchards Battle">"Congdon Orchards Battle</a> sees IWW
  members fight police as the IWW tries to end child labour and gain an
  "<a   href="Eight-Hour_Workday.md" class="wikilink"
  title="eight-hour workday">"eight-hour workday</a> alongside wage
  increases in Yakima Valley, Washington, USA.
- 1937: The IWW and AWO fight against farm owners in the
  "<a   href="Spinach_Riot_(1937).md" class="wikilink"
  title="Spinach Riot">"Spinach Riot</a>.

### 1940s

- 1946: The IWW participates in a
  "<a   href="Schrimer-Dornbirer_Strike_(1946).md" class="wikilink"
  title="strike">"strike</a> at the Schrimer-Dornbirer in Cleveland,
  Ohio, USA and wins 45 cent/hour pay boost in Cleveland.[^1]
- 1949: The IWW is placed on the US Attorney General's List of
  Subversive Organizations.[^2]

### 1950s

- 1950: Cleveland branches of the IWW withdraw after an IWW referendum
  refuses to sign the "<a   href="Taft-Hartley_Act.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Taft-Hartley Act">"Taft-Hartley Act</a>.
- 1955: The IWW turns 50 and is nearing extinction.[^3]
- 1959: Organizing campaign among restaurant workers and greenhouse
  workers in New York City.

### 1960s

- 1964: IWW members assist in organizing the
  "<a   href="Mount_Isa_Mines_Strike_(1964).md" class="wikilink"
  title="Mount Isa Mines Strike">"Mount Isa Mines Strike</a>.
- 1967: IWW referendum votes to allow students to join IWW as members of
  Educational Workers.[^4]

### 1970s

- 1971: MTW branch established among dockworkers in Malmo, Sweden.[^5]
- 1977: The IWW organizes bus drivers and washers in Santa Cruz,
  California, USA and wins higher wages, health and dental benefits,
  safer working conditions, grievance procedures, legal insurance, paid
  holidays and vacations, 32 hours' work for 40 hours' pay, retirement
  benefits, profit sharing, and the elimination of sexual, racial and
  other forms of discrimination due to 100% of workers signing up.[^6]

### 1980s

### 1990s

- 1995: San Francisco and Santa Cruz IWW members establish the
  [iww.org](https://www.iww.org) server and website. The IWW is only the
  second labor union in the world (the first was an Israeli teacher's
  union local) with a website and the first international union to have
  one.<ref>IWW Chronology (1990 - 1995) -
  <https://www.iww.org/about/chronology/10>

</ref>

- 1997: 3,240 gold miners in
  "<a   href="Republic_of_Sierra_Leone.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Sierra Leone">"Sierra Leone</a> work with the IWW, but the
  "<a   href="Sierra_Leone_Civil_War.md" class="wikilink"
  title="civil war">"civil war</a> forced many to flee to neighbouring
  "<a   href="Republic_of_Guinea.md" class="wikilink" title="Guinea">"Guinea</a>,
  where plans were made to organize metal workers, the current state of
  these workers is unknown.[^7]
- 1997: Anarcho-syndicalist group Solidaarsuus affiliates with IWW and
  launches campaign for 6-hour day to fight unemployment. Includes a
  400-person demonstration in Finland.

## 2000s

- 2004: The IWW organizes the
  "<a   href="Starbucks_Workers_Union.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Starbucks Workers Union">"Starbucks Workers Union</a>, largely
  active in the USA and Canada.
- 2006: The [German Language Membership Regional Organizing
  Committee](https://www.wobblies.org/news/) (GLAMROC) is founded in
  Cologne, Germany to organize workers in Germany, Luxembourg,
  Switzerland and Austria.[^8]

### 2010s

- 2011: The IWW begins (with the assistance of the French CNT) to
  prepare for a general strike across the American Midwest in response
  to the
  "<a   href="Wisconsin_State_Workers&#39;_Protests_(2011).md" class="wikilink"
  title="2011 Wisconsin Protests">"2011 Wisconsin Protests</a>.
- 2012: The Ugandan Regional Organizing Committee (ROC) is formed to
  organize workers across Uganda, but was eventually dissolved after it
  violated its own constitution by allowing employers to join.[^9]
- 2014: The "<a   href="Incarcerated_Workers&#39;_Organizing_Committee.md"
  class="wikilink"
  title="Incarcerated Workers&#39; Organizing Committee">"Incarcerated
  Workers' Organizing Committee</a> (IWOC) is formed to fight US
  prison-industrial complex.
- 2015: The
  "<a   href="Iceland_Regional_Organizing_Committee.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Iceland Regional Organizing Committee">"Iceland Regional
  Organizing Committee</a> (IceROC) is the first branch of the IWW to
  form in "<a   href="Iceland.md" class="wikilink" title="Iceland">"Iceland</a>
  and mainly focus on organizing
  "<a   href="Sex_Work.md" class="wikilink" title="sex workers">"sex workers</a>
  for protection against repression by the police.<ref>Sex Worker-Led
  Organisation formed in Iceland -
  <https://www.nswp.org/news/sex-worker-led-organisation-formed-iceland>

</ref>

- 2015: GLAMROC is reported as having over 200 members in good standing
  as well as having branches in 16 cities.[^10]
- 2016: The IWW organizes the first fast food workers union in US
  history, the "<a   href="Burgerville_Workers_Union.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Burgerville Workers Union">"Burgerville Workers Union</a>.
- 2018: The IWOC organizes a
  "<a   href="2018_US_Prison_Strike.md" class="wikilink"
  title="second general strike">"second general strike</a> across US
  prisons, demanding an end to prison slavery, massive expansion of
  prisoner rights and welfare, an end to racism in US prisons and the
  granting of voting rights to prisoners.
- 2018: The "<a   href="IWW_Couriers&#39;_Network.md" class="wikilink"
  title="IWW Couriers&#39; Network">"IWW Couriers' Network</a> is formed
  in the UK to organise drivers with UberEats and Deliveroo.[^11]
- 2019: IWW Australia reaches 100 members.[^12]
- 2019: IWW organises the
  "<a   href="TEFL_Workers&#39;_Union.md" class="wikilink"
  title="TEFL Workers&#39; Union">"TEFL Workers' Union</a> for teachers
  in the UK.
