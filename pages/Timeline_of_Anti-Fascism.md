This is a timeline of events to do with
"<a   href="Anti-Fascism.md" class="wikilink"
title="anti-fascism">anti-fascism</a> from its origins in the late 1800s
to the modern day.

## 1800s

### 1890s

- 1898: At the height of the
  "<a   href="Dreyfus_Affair.md" class="wikilink" title="Dreyfus Affair">Dreyfus
  Affair</a>, proto-anti-fascists composed of anarchists and socialists
  in France protect pro-Dreyfus speakers and witnesses on their way to
  courthouses and in the streets. They plastered the city with posters
  to reclaim public space from the anti-Semites, staged
  counter-demonstrations and infiltrated meetings to disrupt and spy on
  them. "<a   href="Sébastien_Faure.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Sébastien Faure">Sébastien Faure</a> forged invitations to a
  meeting of the opposition at a local restaurant in Marseille. Upon
  being denied entry, the pro-Dreyfusards went around the side and
  smashed through a glass door to storm in and disrupt the proceedings.

## 1900s

### 1910s

### 1920s

- 1921: The "<a   href="Arditi_Del_Popolo.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Arditi Del Popolo">Arditi Del Popolo</a> is founded in Italy in
  response to Mussolini's growing influence. It is the first militant
  anti-fascist organization and engages in gunfights with fascist gangs.

### 1930s

- 1934: The "<a   href="February_Uprising_(Austria).md" class="wikilink"
  title="February Uprising">February Uprising</a> in Austria sees
  socialist try to stop the rise of fascists in the country.
- 1934: Inspired by the Austrian uprising,
  "<a   href="National_Confederation_of_Labour_(Spain).md" class="wikilink"
  title="CNT-affiliated">CNT-affiliated</a> miners in Asturias,
  "<a   href="Spain.md" class="wikilink" title="Spain">Spain</a> lead an
  "<a   href="Asturias_Uprising.md" class="wikilink"
  title="uprising">uprising</a> to protest the entry of fascists into
  the coalition government.
- 1936: The "<a   href="Spanish_Civil_War.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Spanish Civil War">Spanish Civil War</a> begins as much of the
  Spanish working class attempts to stave off the military coup by
  "<a   href="Francisco_Franco.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Francisco Franco">Francisco Franco</a>.
- 1936: The "<a   href="Battle_of_Cable_Street.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Battle of Cable Street">Battle of Cable Street</a> sees
  anti-fascists battle fascists led by
  "<a   href="Oswald_Mosely.md" class="wikilink" title="Oswald Mosely">Oswald
  Mosely</a> in a Jewish neighbourhood in London, England.

### 1940s

- 1944: Prisoners at Auschwitz
  "<a   href="Auschwitz#Uprising.md" class="wikilink"
  title="launch an uprising">launch an uprising</a> to liberate
  themselves, it fails but destroys the crematoriums.
- 1946: The
  "<a   href="43_Group.md" class="wikilink" title="43 Group">43 Group</a> is
  founded in London by Jewish Veterans of
  "<a   href="World_War_II.md" class="wikilink" title="World War II">World War
  II</a> to fight off followers of Oswald Mosely.

### 1950s

- 1958: The "<a   href="Battle_of_Hayes_Pond.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Battle of Hayes Pond">Battle of Hayes Pond</a> sees 500 Lumbee
  Indigenous Americans end a
  "<a   href="Ku_Klux_Klan.md" class="wikilink" title="KKK">KKK</a> meeting.

### 1960s

- 1962: The
  "<a   href="62_Group.md" class="wikilink" title="62 Group">62 Group</a> is
  founded in London to defend immigrants from the Carribean from
  Britain's growing fascist movement.

### 1970s

- 1974:
  "<a   href="Kevin_Gately.md" class="wikilink" title="Kevin Gately">Kevin
  Gately</a> is killed by police during an anti-fascist march in the UK.
- 1976: The "<a   href="Southall_Youth_Movement.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Southall Youth Movement">Southall Youth Movement</a> in England
  forms in response to the murder of teenager
  "<a   href="Gurdip_Singh_Chaggar.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Gurdip Singh Chaggar">Gurdip Singh Chaggar</a> by fascists.
- 1977: The "<a   href="Battle_of_Lewisham.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Battle of Lewisham">Battle of Lewisham</a> sees LGBT people,
  feminists, anarchists, socialists and anti-racists battle a fascist
  march and police with bricks in Lewisham, England. This leads to the
  creation of the "<a   href="Anti-Nazi_League.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Anti-Nazi League">Anti-Nazi League</a> later that year.

### 1980s

- 1982: The
  "<a   href="Black_Dragons.md" class="wikilink" title="Black Dragons">Black
  Dragons</a> forms in France to protect African youth from fascist
  attacks.
- 1982: The
  "<a   href="Blitz_House.md" class="wikilink" title="Blitz House">Blitz
  House</a> in Norway becomes the basis for a new anti-fascist movement
  as Anti-Fascist Action is formed.
- 1983: The "<a   href="March_for_Equality_and_Against_Racism_(France).md"
  class="wikilink" title="March for Equality and Against Racism">March for
  Equality and Against Racism</a> attempts to replicate the success of
  the "<a   href="Civil_Rights_Movement.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Civil Rights Movement">Civil Rights Movement</a> in France.
- 1985: The "<a   href="Red_Warriors_(France).md" class="wikilink"
  title="Red Warriors">Red Warriors</a> becomes a punk communist
  anti-fascist street gang in France.
- 1985: Anti-fascist
  "<a   href="Günter_Sare.md" class="wikilink" title="Günter Sare">Günter
  Sare</a> is killed by a police water cannon during an anti-racist
  march in West Germany.
- 1986: The Revolutionary Anti-Fascists set fire to a barn celebrating
  Adolf Hitler's 100th birthday in West Germany.
- 1989: "<a   href="Cornelia_Wessmann.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Cornelia Wessmann">Cornelia Wessmann</a> is killed after a riot
  police truck chases her into oncoming traffic at an anti-racist
  demonstration.

### 1990s

- 1992: The "<a   href="Battle_of_Waterloo_(UK).md" class="wikilink"
  title="Battle of Waterloo">Battle of Waterloo</a> sees anti-fascists
  fight neo-nazis punks in England.
- 1995: The "<a   href="Battle_of_Sandaker.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Battle of Sandaker">Battle of Sandaker</a> sees anti-fascists
  fight police and neo-nazis in Sandaker, Norway.
- 1997: The First International Conference on Anti-Fascism is held in
  London.
- 1999:
  "<a   href="Björn_Söderberg.md" class="wikilink" title="Björn Söderberg">Björn
  Söderberg</a> is muredered by neo-nazis in his home in Sweden, leading
  to tens of thousands of people protesting fascists in the country.

## 2000s

### 2010s

- 2011: 10 years of actions by Swedish anti-fascists bring an end to the
  "<a   href="Salem_Marches.md" class="wikilink" title="Salem Marches">Salem
  Marches</a>.
- 2017: The "<a   href="Battle_for_Charlottesville.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Battle for Charlottesville">Battle for Charlottesville</a>
  strongly weakens the growing US fascist movement known as the
  alt-right.
