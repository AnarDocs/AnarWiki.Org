A **timeline of insurrectionary
anarchism.**"<a   href="Insurrectionary_Anarchism.md" class="wikilink"
title="Insurrectionary anarchism">"Insurrectionary anarchism</a> is a
philosophy aiming for a refusal to organise and negotiate with
authorities, instead opting to violently destroy them and their
institutions to liberate humanity.

## 1800s

- 1866: "<a   href="Dmitry_Karakozov.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Dmitry Karakozov">"Dmitry Karakozov</a> made an unsuccessful
  attempt on the life of Tsar Alexander II at the gates of the Summer
  Garden in St Petersburg. As the Tsar was leaving, Dmitry rushed
  forward to fire. The attempt was thwarted by Osip Komissarov, a
  peasant-born hatter's apprentice, who jostled Karakozov's elbow just
  before the shot was fired.
- 1878:
  "<a   href="Max_Hödel.md" class="wikilink" title="Max Hödel">"Max Hödel</a>
  attempts to assassinate Kaiser Wilhelm I of Germany. His two attempts
  to shoot the monarch both fail, and he is apprehended and executed
  later that year.
- 1878: "<a   href="Sergey_Stepnyak-Kravchinsky.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Sergey Stepnyak-Kravchinsky">"Sergey Stepnyak-Kravchinsky</a>
  stabs to death General Nikolai Mezentsov, head of the Tsar's secret
  police, in response to the execution of
  "<a   href="Ivan_Kovalsky.md" class="wikilink" title="Ivan Kovalsky">"Ivan
  Kovalsky</a>.
- 1878: "<a   href="Giovanni_Passannante.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Giovanni Passannante">"Giovanni Passannante</a> attempts to
  assassinate with a dagger King Umberto I of Italy. He is sentenced to
  death, but is commuted to life in prison, where he goes insane and is
  taken to the asylum.
- 1879: "<a   href="Grigori_Goldenberg.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Grigori Goldenberg">"Grigori Goldenberg</a> shoots Prince Dmitri
  Kropotkin (no, not our favourite
  "<a   href="Peter_Kropotkin.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Prince Kropotkin">"Prince Kropotkin</a>), the Governor of
  Kharkov in the Russian Empire, to death.
- 1879: "<a   href="Alexander_Soloviev.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Alexander Soloviev">"Alexander Soloviev</a> attempts to
  assassinate Tsar Alexander II of Russia. The monarch spots the weapon
  in his hands and flees, but Soloviev still fires five shots, all of
  which miss. Soloviev is captured and hanged.
- 1880: "<a   href="Stepan_Khalturin.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Stepan Khalturin">"Stepan Khalturin</a> successfully blows up
  part of the Winter Palace in an attempt to assassinate Tsar Alexander.
  Although the Tsar escapes unharmed, eight soldiers are killed and 45
  wounded. The invention of
  "<a   href="dynamite.md" class="wikilink" title="dynamite">"dynamite</a> had
  "<a   href="Benedict_Anderson.md" class="wikilink"
  title="now arrived politically">"now arrived politically</a>.
- 1881: Tsar Alexander II of Russia is killed by a bombing coordinated
  by "<a   href="Narodnaya_Volya.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Narodnaya Volya">"Narodnaya Volya</a>.
- 1892: "<a   href="Alexander_Berkman.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Alexander Berkman">"Alexander Berkman</a> tries to kill
  capitalist "<a   href="Henry_Clay_Frick.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Henry Clay Frick">"Henry Clay Frick</a> in New York City, USA in
  retaliation for Frick's hiring of
  "<a   href="Pinkerton.md" class="wikilink" title="Pinkerton">"Pinkerton</a>
  detectives to break up the
  "<a   href="Homestead_Strike_(1892).md" class="wikilink"
  title="Homestead Strike">"Homestead Strike</a>, resulting in the deaths
  of seven steelworkers. Although badly wounded, Frick survives, and
  Berkman is arrested and eventually imprisoned for 22 years.
- 1893: Santiago Salvador throws two bombs into a theatre in Barcelona,
  Spain, killing 20 people.
- 1893: "<a   href="Auguste_Vaillant.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Auguste Vaillant">"Auguste Vaillant</a> throws a bomb in the
  French National Assembly, killing nobody and injuring one. He is then
  sentenced to death and executed by the
  "<a   href="guillotine.md" class="wikilink" title="guillotine">"guillotine</a>,
  shouting "Death to bourgeois society and long live anarchy!" before
  his death. He had not intended to kill anyone, but only to injure them
  in retaliation for the execution of
  "<a   href="Ravachol.md" class="wikilink" title="Ravachol">"Ravachol</a>, who
  was executed for four bombings.
- 1894: "<a   href="Émile_Henry.md" class="wikilink" title="Émile Henry">"Émile
  Henry</a>, intending to avenge Auguste Vaillant, sets off a bomb in
  Café Terminus in Paris,
  "<a   href="France.md" class="wikilink" title="France">"France</a> killing
  one and injuring twenty.
- 1894: "<a   href="Sante_Geronimo_Caserio.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Sante Geronimo Caserio">"Sante Geronimo Caserio</a>, seeking
  revenge for Auguste Vaillant and Émile Henry, stabs the President of
  France, to death. He is executed by guilltone later that year.
- 1896: "<a   href="Dimitris_Matsalis.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Dimitris Matsalis">"Dimitris Matsalis</a>, an anarchist
  shoemaker, stabs a banker and merchant, killing one in Greece.
- 1897: "<a   href="Pietro_Acciarito.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Pietro Acciarito">"Pietro Acciarito</a> tries to stab King
  Umberto of Italy. He is jailed for life.
- 1897: "<a   href="Michele_Angiolillo.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Michele Angiolillo">"Michele Angiolillo</a> shoots dead Spanish
  Prime Minister Antonio Cánovas del Castillo at a thermal bath resort,
  seeking vengeance for the imprisonment and torture of alleged
  revolutionaries at the Montjuïc fortress. He is later executed.
- 1898:
  "<a   href="Luigi_Lucheni.md" class="wikilink" title="Luigi Lucheni">"Luigi
  Lucheni</a> stabs to death Empress Elisabeth, the consort of Emperor
  Franz Joseph I of Austria-Hungary, with a needle file in Geneva,
  Switzerland. Lucheni is sentenced to life in prison and eventually
  commits suicide in his cell.

## 1900s

- 1900:
  "<a   href="Gaetano_Bresci.md" class="wikilink" title="Gaetano Bresci">"Gaetano
  Bresci</a> shoots dead King Umberto, in revenge for the
  "<a   href="Bava-Beccaris_Massacre.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Bava-Beccaris massacre">"Bava-Beccaris massacre</a> in Milan,
  Italy. Bresci is sentenced to prison for life on Santo Stefano Island,
  where he is found dead less than a year later.
- 1901:
  "<a   href="Leon_Czolgosz.md" class="wikilink" title="Leon Czolgosz">"Leon
  Czolgosz</a> shoots U.S. President William McKinley at point-blank
  range at the Pan-American Exposition in Buffalo, New York. He is later
  executed by electric chair.
- 1902:
  "<a   href="Gennaro_Rubino.md" class="wikilink" title="Gennaro Rubino">"Gennaro
  Rubino</a> attempts to murder King Leopold II of
  "<a   href="Belgium.md" class="wikilink" title="Belgium">"Belgium</a>. All
  three of Rubino's shots miss the monarch's carriage, and he is quickly
  subdued by the crowd and taken into police custody.
- 1921: Three anarchists on a motorcycle shoot dead Spanish Prime
  Minister Eduardo Dato Iradier in Puerta de Alcalá, Madrid.
- 1926: 15-year old
  "<a   href="Anteo_Zamboni.md" class="wikilink" title="Anteo Zamboni">"Anteo
  Zamboni</a> tries to shoot "<a   href="Benito_Mussolini.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Mussolini">"Mussolini</a> during a parade in Bologna, Italy. He
  misses, and is publicly lynched in a few minutes by nearby fascists.
- 1932: A dynamite-filled package bomb left by
  "<a   href="Galleanism.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Galleanists">"Galleanists</a> destroys Judge Webster Thayer's
  home in Worcester, Massachusetts, injuring his wife and a housekeeper.
  Judge Thayer had presided over the trials of Galleanists
  "<a   href="Nicola_Sacco.md" class="wikilink" title="Sacco">"Sacco</a> and
  "<a   href="Bartolomeo_Vanzetti.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Vanzetti">"Vanzetti</a>.
- 1969: Several parked police cars in Chicago, USA are blown up by the
  "<a   href="Weatherman_Underground.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Weatherman Underground">"Weatherman Underground</a> in response
  for the
  "<a   href="COINTELPRO.md" class="wikilink" title="COINTELPRO">"COINTELPRO</a>
  based assassinations of black revolutionaries
  "<a   href="Fred_Hampton.md" class="wikilink" title="Fred Hampton">"Fred
  Hampton</a> and
  "<a   href="Mark_Clark.md" class="wikilink" title="Mark Clark">"Mark Clark</a>.
- 1982: The
  "<a   href="Squamish_Five.md" class="wikilink" title="Squamish Five">"Squamish
  Five</a> blow up an electrical substation on Vancouver Island, Canada
  in protest of the construction a dam.
- 1982: The Squamish Five attempt to
  "<a   href="Litton_Industries_Bombing_(1982).md" class="wikilink"
  title="blow up a factory producing">"blow up a factory producing</a>
  cruise missiles in Toronto, Canada.
- 1982: The "<a   href="Wimmin&#39;s_Fire_Brigade.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Wimmin&#39;s Fire Brigade">"Wimmin's Fire Brigade</a> burns down
  three pornography stores accused of selling snuff films (pornographic
  films that depict real death and mutilation).
- 1997: "<a   href="Conscientious_Arsonists.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Conscientious Arsonists">"Conscientious Arsonists</a> in Greece
  begin burning 62 cars over a year-long timespan that belong to various
  politicians, corporate executives, journalists and army officers.

## 2000s

- 2000: "<a   href="17N.md" class="wikilink" title="17N">"17N</a> is alleged to
  have assassinated British military attache Stephen Saunders in
  "<a   href="Greece.md" class="wikilink" title="Greece">"Greece</a>, although
  some journalists allege it is a part of
  "<a   href="Operation_Gladio.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Operation Gladio">"Operation Gladio</a>.
