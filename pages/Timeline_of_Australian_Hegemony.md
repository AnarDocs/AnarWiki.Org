## 1900s

- 1901: Following independence from the
  "<a   href="British_Empire.md" class="wikilink" title="British Empire">"British
  Empire</a>, Australia continued to send soldiers to help the British
  Empire during the "<a   href="Second_Boer_War.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Second Boer War">"Second Boer War</a> in South Africa and
  "<a   href="Boxer_Rebellion.md" class="wikilink" title="Boxer Rebellion">"Boxer
  Rebellion</a> in China.
- 1914: Australia assisted the British Empire in
  "<a   href="World_War_I.md" class="wikilink" title="World War I">"World War
  I</a>, seizing "<a   href="Papua_New_Guinea.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Papua New Guinea">"Papua New Guinea</a> from the
  "<a   href="German_Empire.md" class="wikilink" title="German Empire">"German
  Empire</a> and sending many Australians to die in Europe.
- 1916: An armed mob which included officials and residents rounded up a
  large number of Aboriginal men, women and children who were then shot
  near "<a   href="Mowla_Bluff_Massacre_(1916).md" class="wikilink"
  title="Mowla Bluff">"Mowla Bluff</a>. The bodies were burned. 300-400
  people were killed.
- 1926: Several police murder and burn the bodies of 20 aboriginal
  people in Northwest Australia in the
  "<a   href="Forrest_River_Massacre_(1926).md" class="wikilink"
  title="Forrest River">"Forrest River</a> region.
- 1928: Several police lead the
  "<a   href="Coniston_Massacre_(1928).md" class="wikilink"
  title="Coniston Massacre">"Coniston Massacre</a> of aboriginal
  Australians, which kills between 31 and 200 people.
