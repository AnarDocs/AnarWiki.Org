The **Revolutions of 1989 - 1992**(sometimes called the **Fall of
Nations** or the **Autumn of Nations**) refers to a
<a href="Revolutionary_Wave.md" class="wikilink"
title="revolutionary wave">revolutionary wave</a> that occurred near the
end of the 20th century. Unlike other revolutionary waves, it did not
result in major progressive changes or the creation of left-wing
governments. It coincided with the rise of neoliberal economics entering
a much stronger phase and the birth of the early
<a href="internet.md" class="wikilink" title="internet">internet</a>. As a
result of the revolutionary wave, a lot of government corruption and
conspiracies were also exposed.

  government did not have enough money to pay its army. The banking

  title="Czechoslovakia">Czechoslovakia</a> becomes a
  <a href="Representative_Democracy.md" class="wikilink"
  title="representative democracy">representative democracy</a> during

- 1989: An uprising breaks out in
  <a href="East_Germany.md" class="wikilink" title="East Germany">East

  Wall and the reunification with
  <a href="West_Germany.md" class="wikilink" title="West Germany">West

- 1989: Following the fall of Marxist-Leninism,
  <a href="Hungary.md" class="wikilink" title="Hungary">Hungary</a> becomes

  title="representative democracy">representative democracy</a> during
  the <a href="Velvet_Revolution.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Velvet Revolution">Velvet Revolution</a>.
- May 1990: In the aftermath of the 8888 Uprising, the government of
  Myanmar abandons its military socialist government and creates a
  representative democracy.
- July 1990:
  <a href="Belarus.md" class="wikilink" title="Belarus">Belarus</a> leaves
  the USSR and becomes an independent state with a representative
  democracy.
- October 1990: The
  <a href="Mass_Uprising_in_Bangladesh_(1990).md" class="wikilink"
  title="Mass Uprising begins in Bangladesh">Mass Uprising begins in
  Bangladesh</a> against the authoritarian government and workers and
  student strike and riot, resulting in the fall of the government and
  creation of a representative democracy.
- December 1990: Following the fall of Marxist-Leninism,
  <a href="Socialist_Albania.md" class="wikilink" title="Albania">Albania</a>
  becomes a <a href="Representative_Democracy.md" class="wikilink"
  title="representative democracy">representative democracy</a>.
  Although The Communist Party of Albania wins elections and remains in
  power until 1992.
- 1990:
  <a href="Cape_Verde.md" class="wikilink" title="Cape Verde">Cape Verde</a>
  abandons its Marxist-Leninist government and creates a representative
  democracy.
- 1990: Chad's dictator falls as a rebel Christian groups takes over
  with <a href="Libya.md" class="wikilink" title="Libya&#39;s">Libya's</a>
  help.
- 1990: Hundreds of civil servants in the
  <a href="Ivory_Coast.md" class="wikilink" title="Ivory Coast">Ivory
  Coast</a> went on strike, joined by students protesting institutional
  corruption. The unrest forced the government to support multiparty
  democracy.
- 1990: The <a href="Pinochet_Dictatorship.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Pinochet Regime">Pinochet Regime</a> falls in
  <a href="Chile.md" class="wikilink" title="Chile">Chile</a>, paving the
  way to a representative democracy.
- 1990: Riots and strikes break out across
  <a href="Gabon.md" class="wikilink" title="Gabon">Gabon</a> leading to a
  representative democracy.
- 1991: The <a href="Breakup_of_Yugoslavia.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Breakup of Yugoslavia">Breakup of Yugoslavia</a> begins,
  leading to the fall of Europe's last socialist state in one of the
  most violent and racist conflicts since
  <a href="World_War_II.md" class="wikilink" title="World War II">World War
  II</a>, the conflict lasts until 2001.
- April 1991:
  <a href="Georgia.md" class="wikilink" title="Georgia">Georgia</a> becomes
  independent from the USSR and becomes a representative democracy.
- May 1991: Marxist-Leninism falls in
  <a href="Socialist_Angola.md" class="wikilink" title="Angola">Angola</a>
  as the government becomes a representative democracy and reaches a
  temporary peace deal with the rebels to end the
  <a href="Angolan_Civil_War.md" class="wikilink"
  title="ongoing civil war">ongoing civil war</a>. However, the deal
  collapse after the rebels begin to fight the government after losing
  the election.
- May 1991: Representative democracy is introduced in
  <a href="Guinea-Bissau.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Guinea-Bissau">Guinea-Bissau</a>.
- September 1991:
  <a href="Armenia.md" class="wikilink" title="Armenia">Armenia</a> leaves
  the <a href="USSR.md" class="wikilink" title="USSR">USSR</a> to become an
  independent state and becomes a representative democracy.
- October 1991: The <a href="Djiboutian_Civil_War.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Djiboutian Civil War">Djiboutian Civil War</a> begins between
  two ethnic groups.
- December 1991:
  <a href="Azerbaijan.md" class="wikilink" title="Azerbaijan">Azerbaijan</a>
  leaves the USSR and becomes an independent state with a representative
  democracy.
- December 1991: The <a href="Algerian_Civil_War.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Algerian Civil War">Algerian Civil War</a> begins between two
  Islamist groups and the government.
- 1991: <a href="Estonia.md" class="wikilink" title="Estonia">Estonia</a>
  becomes independent from the USSR.
- 1991:
  <a href="Ethiopia.md" class="wikilink" title="Ethiopia">Ethiopia</a>
  abandons its Marxist-Leninist government and creates a representative
  democracy.
- 1992:
  <a href="Cambodia.md" class="wikilink" title="Cambodia">Cambodia</a>
  abandons its Marxist-Leninist government and returns to a
  representative democracy.
- 1992: Pro-democracy activists convince the government of the
  <a href="Central_African_Republic.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Central African Republic">Central African Republic</a> to hold
  elections.
- 1992: Following the fall of Marxist-Leninism, the
  <a href="Republic_of_the_Congo.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Republic of the Congo">Republic of the Congo</a> becomes a
  <a href="Representative_Democracy.md" class="wikilink"
  title="representative democracy">representative democracy</a>.
- 1992: <a href="Guyana.md" class="wikilink" title="Guyana">Guyana</a>
  abandons its authoritarian government and embraces representative
  democracy.
- 1992: <a href="Guinea.md" class="wikilink" title="Guinea">Guinea</a>
  begins to abandon its authoritarianism and transition to a
  representative democracy.
- May 1992: Ghana abandons its authoritarian government and embraces
  representative democracy.

## Uprisings, Strikes and Other Events

- 1989: The <a href="Afghanistan_War.md" class="wikilink"
  title="USSR withdraws from Afghanistan">USSR withdraws from
  Afghanistan</a>, ensuring a victory for the Taliban.
- April 1989: <a href="Belgian_Political_Scandals.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Several political scandals">Several political scandals</a>
  begin to unveil themselves in Belgium ranging from simple corruption
  to assassinations to potential sex trafficking rings.
- April 1989: The
  <a href="Pittston_Coal_Miners&#39;_Strike.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Pittston Coal Miners&#39; Strike">Pittston Coal Miners'
  Strike</a> begins, being one of the last successful miner's strikes in
  US history.
- June 1989: The <a href="Tiananmen_Square_Massacre.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Tiananmen Square Massacre">Tiananmen Square Massacre</a> occurs
  in <a href="China.md" class="wikilink" title="China">China</a> as the
  army shoots workers and students protesting for greater democracy.
- August 1989: The
  <a href="Australian_Pilots&#39;_Dispute.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Australian Pilots&#39; Dispute">Australian Pilots' Dispute</a>
  begins, being the last large-scale strike in
  <a href="Australia.md" class="wikilink" title="Australian">Australian</a>
  history as pilots attempt to go on strike against long hours and low
  pay, leading to the government using air force pilots as scabs to help
  prop up Australia's tourism industry.
- 1989: <a href="Argentinian_Riots_(1989).md" class="wikilink"
  title="Massive riots break out across Argentina">Massive riots break out
  across Argentina</a> in response to an economic crisis involving
  hyperinflations and shortages of consumer goods.
- July 1990: The
  <a href="Oka_Uprising.md" class="wikilink" title="Oka Uprising">Oka
  Uprising</a> occurs, the first of several armed indigenous revolts
  against the
  <a href="Canada.md" class="wikilink" title="Canadian">Canadian</a>
  government.
- 1990: The <a href="Guns_for_Antigua.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Guns for Antigua">Guns for Antigua</a> conspiracy is exposed,
  where the government of <a href="Antigua_and_Barbuda.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Antigua and Barbuda">Antigua and Barbuda</a> had been selling
  off surplus military supplies to drug cartels in
  <a href="Colombia.md" class="wikilink" title="Colombia">Colombia</a>.
- 1991: The <a href="Special_Period_(Cuba).md" class="wikilink"
  title="Special Period">Special Period</a> begins in
  <a href="Cuba.md" class="wikilink" title="Cuba">Cuba</a>.
- 1991: The
  <a href="Dili_Massacre.md" class="wikilink" title="Dili Massacre">Dili
  Massacre</a> occurs in
  <a href="East_Timor.md" class="wikilink" title="East Timor">East Timor</a>.
- January 1992: The <a href="Salvadoran_Civil_War.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Salvadoran Civil War">Salvadoran Civil War</a> ends.
- 1990: Economic crisis in Finland.
- 1991: Coup in Haiti.
- 1991: Economic reforms in India.
