# News from Nowhere

***News from Nowhere*** is an 1890
"<a   href="utopian_socialism.md" class="wikilink"
title="utopian socialist">"utopian socialist</a> novel by
"<a   href="William_Morris.md" class="wikilink" title="William Morris">"William
Morris</a>, originally serialised in the
"<a   href="Socialist_League_(UK,_1885).md" class="wikilink"
title="Socialist League">"Socialist League</a>'s newspaper
*"<a   href="Commonweal_(UK).md" class="wikilink"
title="Commonweal">"Commonweal</a>*. The work presents a vision of a
future "<a   href="anarchist_society.md" class="wikilink"
title="anarchist society">"anarchist society</a> based on
"<a   href="common_ownership.md" class="wikilink"
title="common ownership">"common ownership</a>,
"<a   href="mutual_aid.md" class="wikilink" title="mutual aid">"mutual aid</a>,
and the abolition of the state, private property, and all forms of
authority.

## Overview

The novel follows William Guest, who awakens in a future England
transformed into a decentralised, stateless society. This world has
eliminated not only private property and monetary systems, but also
courts, prisons, marriage laws, formal government, and all hierarchical
authority structures. The society functions through
"<a   href="voluntary_association.md" class="wikilink"
title="voluntary association">"voluntary association</a>,
"<a   href="direct_democracy.md" class="wikilink"
title="direct democracy">"direct democracy</a>, and the principle that
work should be creative and pleasurable rather than coercive.

Morris wrote *News from Nowhere* as a direct response to
"<a   href="Edward_Bellamy.md" class="wikilink" title="Edward Bellamy">"Edward
Bellamy</a>'s *"<a   href="Looking_Backward.md" class="wikilink"
title="Looking Backward">"Looking Backward</a>*, which he criticised for
its authoritarian
"<a   href="state_socialism.md" class="wikilink" title="state socialism">"state
socialism</a> and industrial regimentation. Where Bellamy envisioned a
highly centralised society with military-style organisation, Morris
presented an
"<a   href="anarchist.md" class="wikilink" title="anarchist">"anarchist</a>
alternative emphasising decentralisation, craftsmanship, and harmony
with nature.

## Anarchist Vision

### Abolition of the State

In Morris's utopia, all governmental institutions have withered away.
There are no police, no military, no bureaucracy, and no centralised
authority of any kind. Old Hammond explains to Guest that "we have no
force at all to compel people to work"[^1] - the entire system operates
through voluntary cooperation and
"<a   href="mutual_aid.md" class="wikilink" title="mutual aid">"mutual aid</a>.

The Houses of Parliament have been converted into a dung market,
symbolising the complete rejection of representative democracy in favour
of direct participation. Decision-making occurs through local assemblies
where communities discuss matters affecting them, with no higher
authority to impose solutions.

### Economic Organisation

The society operates on principles of
"<a   href="gift_economy.md" class="wikilink" title="gift economy">"gift
economy</a> and
"<a   href="commons.md" class="wikilink" title="commons">"commons</a>
ownership. Money has been abolished entirely, with goods and services
freely given according to need and ability. Morris addresses the common
objection to anarchism - the supposed lack of work incentive - by
arguing that when work becomes creative and pleasurable rather than wage
labour, people naturally contribute to society.

Production is organised around
"<a   href="workers&#39;_self-management.md" class="wikilink"
title="workers&#39; self-management">"workers' self-management</a>, with
craftspeople working according to their interests and the community's
needs. The division between art and labour has been eliminated, with all
work becoming creative expression.

### Social Relations

Marriage as a legal institution has been abolished, replaced by free
association based on mutual affection. Clara and Dick's relationship
demonstrates how people form and dissolve partnerships freely without
state or religious interference. Children are raised communally,
learning through play and direct engagement with nature rather than
formal schooling.

## Critique of Authority

Morris uses the novel to present a sustained critique of all forms of
authority and hierarchy. The transformation described by Old Hammond
shows how the state, rather than serving the people, primarily existed
to protect private property and maintain class divisions.

The revolution described in "How the Change Came" occurs not through
seizing state power, but through its complete dissolution. Workers
organise themselves into a
"<a   href="federation.md" class="wikilink" title="federation">"federation</a>
that gradually makes the state irrelevant, leading to its collapse
without violence.

## Environmental Vision

The restoration of England's environment represents a key aspect of
Morris's anarchist vision.
"<a   href="Industrial_capitalism.md" class="wikilink"
title="Industrial capitalism">"Industrial capitalism</a> has been
replaced by small-scale, ecological production methods. London's
polluted Thames runs clean, Trafalgar Square has become an apricot
orchard, and the countryside has been restored to natural beauty.

This environmental transformation reflects anarchist principles of
decentralisation and harmony with nature, contrasting sharply with both
capitalist exploitation and state socialist industrialism.

## Response to State Socialism

*News from Nowhere* serves as Morris's explicit rejection of
authoritarian socialism. Where
"<a   href="Edward_Bellamy.md" class="wikilink" title="Edward Bellamy">"Edward
Bellamy</a>'s *Looking Backward* presented a vision of state-controlled
industry and military-style organisation, Morris offered a libertarian
alternative emphasising:

- Decentralised production over industrial regimentation
- Voluntary cooperation over state compulsion
- Craft production over mechanised labour
- Rural communes over industrial cities
- Direct democracy over representative government

Morris argued that true socialism required not just the abolition of
private property, but the complete dismantling of all hierarchical
authority.

## Political Context

The novel emerged from Morris's involvement with the
"<a   href="Socialist_League.md" class="wikilink"
title="Socialist League">"Socialist League</a>, where he debated
anarchist and
"<a   href="Marxist.md" class="wikilink" title="Marxist">"Marxist</a>
tendencies. Originally published in *Commonweal* during 1890, the work
represented Morris's final position in favour of
"<a   href="libertarian_socialism.md" class="wikilink"
title="libertarian socialism">"libertarian socialism</a> and against both
parliamentary politics and state-centred revolution.

Morris's vision influenced later anarchist thinkers and remains a
foundational text of "<a   href="anarchist_literature.md" class="wikilink"
title="anarchist literature">"anarchist literature</a>, demonstrating how
a stateless society might function in practice.

## Influence on Anarchist Thought

*News from Nowhere* has inspired numerous anarchist projects and remains
influential in contemporary
"<a   href="anarchist.md" class="wikilink" title="anarchist">"anarchist</a> and
"<a   href="eco-anarchist.md" class="wikilink"
title="eco-anarchist">"eco-anarchist</a> movements. The work demonstrates
that anarchist society need not be chaotic but can be highly organised
through voluntary cooperation and mutual aid.

The novel's integration of aesthetic, social, and political
transformation continues to influence anarchist approaches to culture,
work, and environmental issues.
