# Timeline of Universal Healthcare

A **timeline of universal healthcare**, mainly focusing on its
introduction in various
<a href="List_of_States" class="wikilink" title="states">states</a>.

## Non-Hierarchal Examples

| Year | Event type | Details | Present time system type (when applicable) | Country |
|----|----|----|----|----|
| 1600s-1900s | Mutual Aid | <a href="Friendly_Societies" class="wikilink"
title="Friendly Societies">Friendly Societies</a> in the UK provide mutual aid and insurance associations, often workplace or community-based, that provide medical insurance. They resist government regulation and face opposition from the medical establishment.[^103] | Mutual Aid | <a href="United_Kingdom" class="wikilink" title="United Kingdom">United
Kingdom</a> |
| 1800s-1900s | Mutual Aid | Welsh miners develop comprehensive mutual aid healthcare systems through collective contributions and democratic control, pooling resources to employ doctors and establish medical facilities for their communities. These systems directly influence the formation of Britain's NHS.[^104] | Mutual Aid | <a href="United_Kingdom" class="wikilink" title="United Kingdom">United
Kingdom</a> |
| 1926-1950 | Experiment | <a href="The_Peckham_Health_Centre" class="wikilink"
title="The Peckham Health Centre">The Peckham Health Centre</a> operates in London, focusing on promoting health and well-being rather than treating disease, with principles of family-centred membership, small weekly subscription, and autonomous administration with "no guiding planners, no cliques, no closed doors, no hierarchies".[^105] | Community Control | <a href="United_Kingdom" class="wikilink" title="United Kingdom">United
Kingdom</a> |
| 1936-1939 | Revolution | During the <a href="Spanish_Revolution" class="wikilink"
title="Spanish Revolution">Spanish Revolution</a>, the CNT Federation of Health Workers comprising 40,000 members establishes a comprehensive health service in Catalonia, providing universal healthcare through socialised hospitals and health centres linked in federations operating on libertarian principles.[^106] | Worker Collective | <a href="Spain" class="wikilink" title="Spain">Spain</a> |
| 1969 | Mutual Aid | The Los Angeles <a href="Black_Panther_Party" class="wikilink"
title="Black Panther Party">Black Panther Party</a>'s Free Clinic is established, co-founded by Black nurse and activist Marie Branch, demonstrating nursing freed from payment models and elitist structures.[^107] | Community Control | <a href="United_States" class="wikilink" title="United States">United
States</a> |
| 2006 | Innovation | <a href="Buurtzorg_Nederland" class="wikilink"
title="Buurtzorg Nederland">Buurtzorg Nederland</a> founded in the Netherlands by nurses dissatisfied with traditional hierarchical healthcare delivery, operating through self-governing teams of nurses who take complete responsibility for patients in their neighborhood without traditional management oversight.[^108] | Self-Management | <a href="Netherlands" class="wikilink"
title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a> |
|  |  |  |  |  |
