![](1280px-Anarchist_flag.svg.png "1280px-Anarchist_flag.svg.png") A
**timeline of "<a   href="Anarcho-Syndicalism.md" class="wikilink"
title="Anarcho-Syndicalism">Anarcho-Syndicalism</a>**, the movement
aiming to establish an anarchist society through trade unionism and
strikes.

## 1900s

- 1901: The
  "<a   href="Argentine_Regional_Workers&#39;_Federation.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Argentine Regional Workers&#39; Federation">Argentine Regional
  Workers' Federation</a> is founded in Buenos Aires and gains a strong
  anarcho-syndicalist influence.
- 1903:
  "<a   href="Sam_Mainwaring.md" class="wikilink" title="Sam Mainwaring">Sam
  Mainwaring</a> coins the term 'anarcho-syndicalism' in pamphlets
  criticising reformist trade unionism.
- 1905: The "<a   href="Industrial_Workers_of_the_World.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Industrial Workers of the World">Industrial Workers of the
  World</a> is founded in Chicago, USA, although it is still debated
  whether or not it is an anarcho-syndicalist organisation.
- 1906: The "<a   href="Brazilian_Workers_Confederation.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Brazilian Workers Confederation">Brazilian Workers
  Confederation</a> is established in Rio de Janeiro.
- 1910: The
  "<a   href="Central_Organisation_of_the_Workers_of_Sweden.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Central Organisation of the Workers of Sweden">Central
  Organisation of the Workers of Sweden</a> is established in Sweden
  after the failure of the
  "<a   href="Swedish_General_Strike_(1909).md" class="wikilink"
  title="Swedish General Strike in 1909">Swedish General Strike in
  1909</a>.
- 1912: The "<a   href="Italian_Syndicalist_Union.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Italian Syndicalist Union">Italian Syndicalist Union</a> is
  established in Modena.
- 1916: The "<a   href="Norwegian_Syndicalist_Federation.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Norwegian Syndicalist Federation">Norwegian Syndicalist
  Federation</a> is established in Oslo.
- 1919: The "<a   href="Vaga_de_La_Canadenca.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Vaga de La Canadenca">Vaga de La Canadenca</a> strike in
  Barcelona forces the Spanish government to establish an 8-hour workday
  after anarcho-syndicalists shut down 70% of the cities economy.
- 1919: The
  "<a   href="Bienno_Rosso.md" class="wikilink" title="Bienno Rosso">Bienno
  Rosso</a> sees anarcho-syndicalists lead workplace occupations and
  create workers' councils.
- 1919: The
  "<a   href="Free_Workers&#39;_Union_of_Germany.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Free Workers&#39; Union of Germany">Free Workers' Union of
  Germany</a> is established after the merge of Free Association of
  German Trade Unions and several small anarcho-syndicalist groups in
  the Ruhr region.
- 1922: The
  "<a   href="International_Workers&#39;_Association.md" class="wikilink"
  title="International Workers&#39; Association">International Workers'
  Association</a> (IWA) is established at an illegal conference in
  Berlin, Germany by delegates from the Italian Syndicalist Union,
  Argentine Regional Workers' Federation, General Confederation of
  Workers in Portugal, Free Workers' Union of Germany, Committee for the
  Defense of Revolutionary Syndicalism in France, Federation du
  Combattant from Paris, Central Organisation of the Workers of Sweden,
  National Labor Secretariat in the Netherlands, the IWW in Chile and
  the Union for Syndicalist Propaganda in Denmark.
- 1922: "<a   href="Guayaquil_General_Strike_(1922).md" class="wikilink"
  title="Guayaquil General Strike">Guayaquil General Strike</a>
- 1924: The Italian Syndicalist Union is driven underground by the
  fascist regime.
- 1926: The
  "<a   href="National_Libertarian_Federation_of_Trade_Unions_(Japan).md"
  class="wikilink"
  title="National Libertarian Federation of Trade Unions">National
  Libertarian Federation of Trade Unions</a> is established in Tokyo,
  Japan.
- 1928: The
  "<a   href="Continental_Association_of_American_Workers.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Continental Association of American Workers">Continental
  Association of American Workers</a> is founded in Buenos Aires,
  Argentina to form a broad anarcho-syndicalist movement across Latin
  America.
- 1933: The Free Workers' Union of Germany is disbanded in response to
  the rise of the
  "<a   href="Nazi_Germany.md" class="wikilink" title="Nazis">Nazis</a>,
  although underground groups begin resistance to the regime.
- 1936: The Spanish Civil War breaks out and anarcho-syndicalists
  "<a   href="Spanish_Revolution.md" class="wikilink"
  title="begin to collectivise">begin to collectivise</a> industry,
  agriculture, transport and utilities
  "<a   href="Revolutionary_Spain.md" class="wikilink"
  title="in a third of the country">in a third of the country</a>.
- 1946: The
  "<a   href="National_Confederation_of_Labour_(France).md" class="wikilink"
  title="National Confederation of Labour">National Confederation of
  Labour</a> is founded in Paris, France by CNT members from Spain and
  former anarcho-syndicalists in the CGT.
- 1950: The "<a   href="Solidarity_Federation_(UK).md" class="wikilink"
  title="Syndicalist Workers&#39; Federation">Syndicalist Workers'
  Federation</a> is founded in the UK by former members of the Anarchist
  Federation.
- 1950: The Italian Syndicalist Union is refounded.
- 1951: The IWA is reestablished at a conference in Toulouse, France.
- 1954: A second "<a   href="Libertarian_League_(NYC).md" class="wikilink"
  title="Libertarian League">Libertarian League</a> is established in
  New York City, USA centered around anarchist bookshops
- 1956: The Central Organisation of the Workers of Sweden leaves the IWA
  over disputes over the SACs running of a state-funded unemployment
  benefits scheme. This nearly destroys the IWA.
- 1977: The "<a   href="Free_Women&#39;s_and_Workers&#39;_Union_(Germany).md"
  class="wikilink" title="Free Women&#39;s and Workers&#39; Union">Free
  Women's and Workers' Union</a> is established in Germany, inspired by
  the reconstruction of the CNT in Spain. It sees itself as the
  successor to the Free Workers' Union of Germany.
- 1979: The
  "<a   href="General_Confederation_of_Labor_(Spain).md" class="wikilink"
  title="General Confederation of Labor">General Confederation of
  Labor</a> splits from the CNT in Spain over issues of taking
  government subsidies and participating in union elections.
- 1979: The Syndicalist Workers' Federation changes its name to the
  Direct Action Movement.
- 1984: The
  "<a   href="Workers&#39;_Solidarity_Alliance_(USA).md" class="wikilink"
  title="Workers&#39; Solidarity Alliance">Workers' Solidarity
  Alliance</a> is established in the USA.
- 1985: The Brazilian Workers Confederation is reestablished.
- 1986: The
  "<a   href="Anarcho-Syndicalist_Federation_(Australia).md" class="wikilink"
  title="Anarcho-Syndicalist Federation">Anarcho-Syndicalist
  Federation</a> is founded in Sydney, Australia, by Bulgarian and
  Spanish exiles with some Australian anarchists.
- 1989: The "<a   href="Awareness_League_(Nigeria).md" class="wikilink"
  title="Awareness League">Awareness League</a> is founded in Nigeria.
- 1994: The Direct Action Movement in the UK changes its name to the
  Solidarity Federation.
- 1995: The
  "<a   href="Confederation_of_Revolutionary_Anarcho-Syndicalists_(Russia).md"
  class="wikilink"
  title="Confederation of Revolutionary Anarcho-Syndicalists">Confederation
  of Revolutionary Anarcho-Syndicalists</a> is established in Moscow,
  Russia as anarcho-communist members split from the Confederation of
  Anarcho-Syndicalists.
- 1996: The Awareness League joins the IWA as the Nigerian section.

## 2000s

- 2006: The SAC organises
  "<a   href="Malmö26.md" class="wikilink" title="Malmö26">Malmö26</a> to
  protest the unjust dismissal of a cook from a sushi restaurant.
- 2009: "<a   href="Libertære_Socialister.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Libertære Socialister">Libertære Socialister</a> is founded in
  Denmark, holding an estimated 300 members across three cities, they
  espouse an anarcho-communist and anarcho-syndicalist position.
- 2010: The "<a   href="Federation_of_Brazilian_Revolutionary_Syndicalist_Organizations.md"
  class="wikilink"
  title="Federation of Brazilian Revolutionary Syndicalist Organizations">Federation
  of Brazilian Revolutionary Syndicalist Organizations</a> is founded to
  offer an anarcho-syndicalist option for Brazil.
- 2015: The Anarcho-Syndicalist Federation is founded in Bangladesh.
- 2017: Libertære Socialister is dissolved after a period of inactivity.
- 2018: The
  "<a   href="International_Confederation_of_Labor.md" class="wikilink"
  title="International Confederation of Labor">International Confederation
  of Labor</a> is established at a conference in Italy from
  organisations that left the IWA, arguing for a more syndicalist
  approach for anarchism.
