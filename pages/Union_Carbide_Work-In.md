The **Union Carbide Work-In** was an experiment in
"<a   href="Workers&#39;_Self-Management.md" class="wikilink"
title="workers&#39; control">"workers' control</a> in Melbourne,
"<a   href="Australia.md" class="wikilink" title="Australia">"Australia</a> in
"<a   href="Timeline_of_Anarchism_in_Oceania.md" class="wikilink"
title="1979">"1979</a>. After
"<a   href="Union_Carbide.md" class="wikilink" title="Union Carbide">"Union
Carbide</a> announced it would fire union workers in response to a
"<a   href="Shorter-Time_Movement.md" class="wikilink"
title="35-hour-workweek campaign">"35-hour-workweek campaign</a>, the
workers took over the Altona Petrochemical Complex (owned by Union
Carbide) and ran it for 51 days. Ending the campaign after they agreed
to rehire all the workers.[^1]
