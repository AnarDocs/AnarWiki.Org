A timeline of the creation and maintenance of the
<a href="French_Empire" class="wikilink" title="French Empire">French
Empire</a>.

## 1500s

- 1555: France attempts to create a colony in Rio de Janerio, now
  <a href="Brazil" class="wikilink" title="Brazil">Brazil</a> called
  'French Antarctique'
- 1562: France attempts to create a colony in Florida, now USA called
  Fort Caroline.
- 1565: France abandons Fort Caroline after a conflict with the
  <a href="Spanish_Empire" class="wikilink" title="Spanish Empire">Spanish
  Empire</a>.
- 1567: France abandons French Antarctique.

## 1600s

- 1605: France colonizes Nova Scotia, now
  <a href="Canada" class="wikilink" title="Canada">Canada</a>.
- 1608: France colonizes Quebec, now Canada.
- 1624: France colonists
  <a href="French_Guiana" class="wikilink" title="French Guiana">French
  Guiana</a>.
- 1624: France establishes trading posts on the coast of
  <a href="Senegal" class="wikilink" title="Senegal">Senegal</a>.
- 1625: France colonizes part of
  <a href="Saint_Kitts_and_Nevis" class="wikilink"
  title="Saint Kitts">Saint Kitts</a>, shared with the British Empire.
- 1635: France colonizes Guadelope and Martinique.
- 1650: France colonizes
  <a href="Saint_Lucia" class="wikilink" title="Saint Lucia">Saint
  Lucia</a>.
- 1660: France begins the
  <a href="Carib_Expulsion" class="wikilink" title="Carib Expulsion">Carib
  Expulsion</a>, involving killing, enslaving or deporting the remaining
  survivors to Dominica and
  <a href="Saint_Vincent_and_the_Grenadines" class="wikilink"
  title="Saint Vincent">Saint Vincent</a>.
- 1664: France colonizes
  <a href="Haiti" class="wikilink" title="Haiti">Haiti</a>.
- 1664: France colonizes Réunion.
- 1673: France colonizes parts of India, establishing the city of
  Chandernagore.
- 1674: France colonizes Pondichéry in India.
- 1680: France colonizes Illinois, now USA.

## 1700s

- 1713: Following the War of the Spanish Succession, France is forced to
  give away Nova Scotia, Newfoundland and Saint Kitts to the British
  Empire.
- 1718: France colonizes
  <a href="Mauritius" class="wikilink" title="Mauritius">Mauritius</a>.
- 1723: France colonizes Yanam in India.
- 1725: France colonizes Mahe in India.
- 1739: France colonizes Karikal in India.
- 1756: France colonizes the
  <a href="Seychelles" class="wikilink" title="Seychelles">Seychelles</a>.
- 1791: The Haitian Revolution begins as the first major anti-colonial
  revolt against the French.

## 1800s

- 1804:
- 1815: France has its colonies returned to it by Britain, at this point
  consisting of Guadelope, Martinique, French Guiana, trading posts in
  Senegal, Réunion and France's cities in India. However, the British
  Empire claims the Seychelles, Mauritius, Tobago and Saint Lucia.
- 1830: France seizes the city of Algiers from the
  <a href="Ottoman_Empire" class="wikilink" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman
  Empire</a>.
- 1884: France colonies
  <a href="Vietnam" class="wikilink" title="Vietnam">Vietnam</a>.
- 1893: France colonies
  <a href="Laos" class="wikilink" title="Laos">Laos</a>.

## 1900s

- 1945: French colonial police
  <a href="Setif_and_Guelma_Massacre" class="wikilink"
  title="massacre">massacre</a> Algerian protesters, killing thousands
  of people.
- 1945: An anti-colonial revolution begins in Vietnam as rebels led by
  <a href="Ho_Chi_Minh" class="wikilink" title="Ho Chi Minh">Ho Chi
  Minh</a> fight for an independent Vietnam.
- 1947: France brutally <a href="Malagasy_Uprising" class="wikilink"
  title="suppresses an attempted anti-colonial revolt">suppresses an
  attempted anti-colonial revolt</a> in Madagascar.
- 1954: France decolonizes Vietnam as the country is split into two.
- 1954: The <a href="Algerian_Revolution" class="wikilink"
  title="Algerian Revolution">Algerian Revolution</a> begins as the most
  famous anti-colonial revolt in the French Empire.
- 1956: France decolonizes
  <a href="Tunisia" class="wikilink" title="Tunisia">Tunisia</a>.
- 1958: France decolonizes
  <a href="Guinea" class="wikilink" title="Guinea">Guinea</a>.
- 1960: France decolonizes its colonies in Africa, leading to the
  formation of
  <a href="Cameroon" class="wikilink" title="Cameroon">Cameroon</a>,
  <a href="Togo" class="wikilink" title="Togo">Togo</a>,
  <a href="Madagascar" class="wikilink" title="Madagascar">Madagascar</a>,
  <a href="Mali" class="wikilink" title="Mali">Mali</a>,
  <a href="Senegal" class="wikilink" title="Senegal">Senegal</a>,
  <a href="Benin" class="wikilink" title="Benin">Benin</a>,
  <a href="Niger" class="wikilink" title="Niger">Niger</a>,
  <a href="Burkina_Faso" class="wikilink" title="Burkina Faso">Burkina
  Faso</a>,
  <a href="Ivory_Coast" class="wikilink" title="Ivory Coast">Ivory
  Coast</a>, <a href="Chad" class="wikilink" title="Chad">Chad</a>,
  <a href="Central_African_Republic" class="wikilink"
  title="Central African Republic">Central African Republic</a>,
  <a href="Republic_of_the_Congo" class="wikilink"
  title="Republic of the Congo">Republic of the Congo</a>,
  <a href="Gabon" class="wikilink" title="Gabon">Gabon</a> and
  <a href="Mauritania" class="wikilink" title="Mauritania">Mauritania</a>.
- 1962: France withdraws from
  <a href="Algeria" class="wikilink" title="Algeria">Algeria</a> as the
  country becomes independent.
- 1975: France decolonizes the
  <a href="Comoros" class="wikilink" title="Comoros">Comoros</a>.
- 1977: France decolonizes
  <a href="Djibouti" class="wikilink" title="Djibouti">Djibouti</a>.
- 1980: France decolonizes
  <a href="Vanuatu" class="wikilink" title="Vanuatu">Vanuatu</a>,
  becoming its last act of decolonisation.
- 1987: France participates in the coup that ousts
  <a href="Thomas_Sankara" class="wikilink" title="Thomas Sankara">Thomas
  Sankara</a> from being leader of
  <a href="Burkina_Faso" class="wikilink" title="Burkina Faso">Burkina
  Faso</a>.
