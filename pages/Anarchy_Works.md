**Anarchy Works** is a book by
"<a   href="Peter_Gelderloos.md" class="wikilink"
title="Peter Gelderloos">"Peter Gelderloos</a> that explains how an
anarchist society would work and answers common objections. It was
published in 2010.

## Summary

It is divided into 8 main chapters, all exploring a different aspect of
anarchism and anarchist theory.

### 1. Human Nature

The first chapter challenges 5 common claims about human nature - that
humans are inherently selfish, competitive, patriarchal, warlike and
authoritarian with a range of historical examples and citing
anthropological research.

### 2. Decisions

The second chapter explores how decisions will be made and enforced as
well as how disputes will be settled.

### 3. Economy

The third chapter outlines anarchist approaches to economics. Answering
questions around the incentive to work, the need for experts, waste
management, caring for the elderly, healthcare, education, technology,
exchange, consumerism, large infrastructure, cities and catastrophes.

### 4. Environment

The fourth chapter outlines anarchist approaches to environmental
protection, such as what stops people destroying the environment and how
to deal with global environmental problems.

### 5. Crime

The fifth chapter outlines how a society without police would work, and
deal with challenges such as gangs and domestic violence.

### 6. Revolution

The sixth chapter outlines an anarchist revolution. Looking at how
anarchists can overcome the state, how to prevent revolutionaries become
new tyrants, reparations and the spreading of an anarchist ethos.

### 7. Neighboring Societies

The seventh chapter looks at how an anarchist society would deal with
neighboring ones, such as defense, societies with un-anarchist values
and how to prevent constant warfare.

### 8. The Future

The eighth and final chapter outlines how to prevent the re-emergence of
the state.
