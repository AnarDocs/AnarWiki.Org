## History

### Founding

The CNT was founded in 1910 as a combination of the growth of
anarcho-syndicalism in Spain and many workers hostility to the socialist
UGT union. It was planned to be founded in 1909, but pushed back the
conference in response to massacres.

### Spanish Civil War

The CNT oversaw the collectivization of farmland, factories, railways,
docks, trams and the organisation of various militias.

### Repression and Survival

After the Spanish Civil War, the CNT was outlawed and its assets
expropriated by the new government. Roughly
<a href="White_Terror_(Spain).md" class="wikilink"
title="180,000 members of the CNT were executed by the government">180,000
members of the CNT were executed by the government</a> during
<a href="World_War_II.md" class="wikilink" title="World War II">World War
II</a>, causing many members to flee the country. Many CNT members lived
in other countries in exile, and others joined the
<a href="Spanish_Maquis.md" class="wikilink" title="Spanish Maquis">Spanish
Maquis</a> to fight the new fascist government, or else others just
stayed quiet. In 1960, the Libertarian Movement in Exile (MLE) was
formed by members of the CNT, FAI and FIJL, holding a conference in
Limoges, Southwest
<a href="France.md" class="wikilink" title="France">France</a>.
Anarcho-syndicalism was nearly destroyed as a political force, but
remained somewhat popular with non-fascist catholics.[^1]
