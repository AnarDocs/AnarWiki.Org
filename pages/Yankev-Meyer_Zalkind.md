**Yankev-Meyer Zalkind** (1875 - 1937) was a
"<a   href="Judaism.md" class="wikilink" title="Jewish Orthodox rabbi">Jewish
Orthodox rabbi</a>, an "<a   href="Anarcho-Communism.md" class="wikilink"
title="anarcho-communist">anarcho-communist</a> and a close friend of
"<a   href="Rudolf_Rocker.md" class="wikilink" title="Rudolf Rocker">Rudolf
Rocker</a>.

## Life

Yankev was born in the Russian Empire to a family of famous merchants
and rabbis. He was a highly successful
"<a   href="student.md" class="wikilink" title="student">student</a> and went
on to study
"<a   href="Language.md" class="wikilink" title="linguistics">linguistics</a>
and philosophy in Germany, France and Switzerland and became able to
speak over 20 languages.[^1]

After graduating, Yank left for Britain and served as rabbi of Cardiff,
Wales and was an active
"<a   href="Zionism.md" class="wikilink" title="Zionist">Zionist</a>. In 1913
he formed a London Estate and began buying land in Israel with the hopes
of setting up Jewish communities in the area.[^2]

During World War I he underwent a spiritual transformation and began to
advocate for
"<a   href="anarchism.md" class="wikilink" title="anarchism">anarchism</a>. In
1916 he left Israel in order to go to London as a
"<a   href="Anti-War_Movement.md" class="wikilink"
title="peace campaigner">peace campaigner</a>. He befriended
"<a   href="Rudolf_Rocker.md" class="wikilink" title="Rudolf Rocker">Rudolf
Rocker</a> in this time and began to advocate for the creation of an
"<a   href="List_of_Anarchist_Societies.md" class="wikilink"
title="anarchist society">anarchist society</a> in the Levant which
could shelter the world's refugees.[^3]

He also became a prominent
hebrew-"<a   href="language.md" class="wikilink" title="language">language</a>
writer, writing Jewish childrens books and attempted to translate the
Talmud into Yiddish.[^4] He also argued rigorously that the logical
conclusion of the teachers of the Talmud was anarchism.[^5] He moved
back to Israel in 1933 and died in Haifa in 1937.[^6]
