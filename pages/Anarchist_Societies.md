Anarchist societies are communities and civilizations that have
organized themselves without hierarchical structures, centralized
states, or coercive authority. Contrary to common assumptions that
hierarchy is necessary for large-scale human organization, extensive
historical and anthropological evidence demonstrates that
non-hierarchical societies have existed throughout human history—from
prehistoric settlements to contemporary movements.

These societies have employed various forms of consensus
decision-making, federation, and mutual aid to coordinate collective
action, resolve disputes, and manage resources. Books such as
<a href="Anarchy_In_Action.md" class="wikilink"
title="Anarchy In Action">Anarchy In Action</a>,
<a href="The_Dawn_Of_Everything.md" class="wikilink"
title="The Dawn Of Everything">The Dawn Of Everything</a> and
<a href="Mutual_Aid:_A_Factor_of_Evolution.md" class="wikilink"
title="Mutual Aid">Mutual Aid</a> detail dozens of such civilizations
and societies which have existed from prehistorical times to the
present, challenging the narrative that states and hierarchies are
inevitable features of human social organization. The examples below
demonstrate the diversity and viability of anarchist principles across
different cultures, time periods, and scales of organization.

**Historical Examples:**

- <a href="Celtic_Ireland.md" class="wikilink" title="Celtic Ireland">Celtic
  Ireland</a> (650-1650) - stateless society with rotating leadership
  and restorative justice
- <a href="Frisian_Freedom.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Frisian Freedom">Frisian Freedom</a> (800–1523) - autonomous
  communes rejecting feudal hierarchy
- <a href="Icelandic_Commonwealth.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Icelandic Commonwealth">Icelandic Commonwealth</a> (930-1262) -
  competitive governance without centralized authority
- <a href="Haudenosaunee.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Haudenosaunee">Haudenosaunee</a> Confederacy (1142-1450) -
  federated democracy with women's councils
- <a href="Taborite_communes.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Taborites">Taborites</a> (1420–1452) - communist society
  abolishing private property
- <a href="Republic_of_Cospaia.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Republic of Cospaia">Republic of Cospaia</a> (1440–1826) -
  stateless territory with communitarian economics
- <a href="Zomia.md" class="wikilink" title="Zomia">Zomia</a> (1500 - 1946
  AD) - highland peoples avoiding state control
- <a href="South_Carolina_Commune.md" class="wikilink"
  title="South Carolina Commune">South Carolina Commune</a>
  (1868-1874) - Black-led Reconstruction government implementing radical
  economic reforms
- <a href="Paris_Commune.md" class="wikilink" title="Paris Commune">Paris
  Commune</a> (France, 1871) - workers' direct democracy and federation
- <a href="Shinmin_Prefecture.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Shinmin Autonomous Region">Shinmin Autonomous Region</a> (S
  Korea, 1929-31) - rural anarchist revolution
- <a href="Revolutionary_Spain.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Revolutionary Catalonia">Revolutionary Catalonia</a>
  (1936-1939) - worker collectives and libertarian socialism
- <a href="Revolutionary_Ukraine.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Free Territory of Ukraine">Free Territory of Ukraine</a>
  (1918-1921) - Makhnovist anarchist experiment

**Current Examples:**

- <a href="Rojava.md" class="wikilink" title="Rojava">Rojava</a> (Northern
  Syria, 2012-present) - democratic confederalism with women's
  liberation
- <a href="Venezuelan_communes.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Venezuelan communes">Venezuelan communes</a> (2006-present) -
  grassroots participatory democracy
- <a href="Abahlali_baseMjondolo.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Abahlali baseMjondolo">Abahlali baseMjondolo</a> (South
  Africa, 2005) - shack dwellers' autonomous movement
- <a href="Zapatista-run_Chiapas.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Zapatista communities">Zapatista communities</a> (Mexico,
  1994-present) - indigenous autonomy and direct democracy - also see
  <a href="Cherán.md" class="wikilink" title="Cherán">Cherán</a> (2011+) &
  Villa de
  <a href="Zaachila.md" class="wikilink" title="Zaachila">Zaachila</a>
  (2006+) for other Mexican examples
- <a href="Spezzano_Albanese.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Spezzano Albanese">Spezzano Albanese</a> (Italy,
  1992-present) - anarchist municipal governance experiment
- <a href="FEJUVE.md" class="wikilink" title="FEJUVE">FEJUVE</a> / El Alto
  (Bolivia, 1979) - federation of neighborhood councils practicing
  indigenous democracy
- <a href="Marinaleda.md" class="wikilink" title="Marinaleda">Marinaleda</a>
  (Spain, 1979-present) - cooperative economy with shared land ownership
- <a href="Freetown_Christiania.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Freetown Christiania">Freetown Christiania</a> (Denmark,
  1971-present) - autonomous neighborhood with collective ownership
- <a href="Twin_Oaks.md" class="wikilink" title="Twin Oaks">Twin Oaks</a>
  (USA, 1967-present) - egalitarian community with labor credit system
- <a href="Sarvodaya_Shramadana_Movement.md" class="wikilink"
  title="Sarvodaya Shramadana Movement">Sarvodaya Shramadana Movement</a>
  (Sri Lanka, 1958-present) - village-level self-reliance and democracy
- The
  <a href="Hutterites.md" class="wikilink" title="Hutterites">Hutterites</a>
  & Bruderhof (1528-present) - federated communes practicing common
  ownership
