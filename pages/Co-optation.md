**Co-optation** or **recuperation** is a concept developed by the
<a href="Situationism" class="wikilink"
title="Situationists">Situationists</a> to explain why social movements
often fail to deliver on their promises. It talks about how various
forms of power are able to destroy threats to them by integrating the
movement into power itself.

<a href="Hijacking" class="wikilink" title="Hijacking">Hijacking</a>
involves flipping the roles, where social movements take cultural
concepts from the power structures.

## Historical Examples

- 1980s: The
  <a href="Kraakers" class="wikilink" title="Kraakers">Kraakers</a> were
  co-opted by social democrats who offered to give squats to the
  Kraakers as their own homes in order to prevent unrest. (Netherlands)
- 2001: The <a href="Argentinazo" class="wikilink"
  title="Argentinazo">Argentinazo</a> was co-opted by Peronist political
  parties into voting for them. (Argentina)
