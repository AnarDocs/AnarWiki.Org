The **Free Women's and Workers' Union** (**FAU**) is
<a href="Confederation" class="wikilink"
title="federation">federation</a> of
<a href="Anarcho-Syndicalism" class="wikilink"
title="anarcho-syndicalist">anarcho-syndicalist</a>
<a href="Trade_Union" class="wikilink" title="unions">unions</a> based
in <a href="Germany" class="wikilink" title="Germany">Germany</a>.

## History

### Founding

Founded in <a href="Timeline_of_Anarchism_in_Germany" class="wikilink"
title="1977">1977</a> after the re-emergence of the
<a href="National_Confederation_of_Labour_(Spain)" class="wikilink"
title="CNT">CNT</a> in the
<a href="Second_Spanish_Revolution" class="wikilink"
title="Second Spanish Revolution">Second Spanish Revolution</a>, the new
FAU saw itself as the successor of the
<a href="Free_Workers&#39;_Union_of_Germany" class="wikilink"
title="Free Workers&#39; Union of Germany">Free Workers' Union of
Germany</a>, which broke up in 1933 to avoid persecution from the
<a href="Nazi_Germany" class="wikilink" title="new government">new
government</a>.

### Major Actions

In 2007, FAU members organised a
<a href="bicycle" class="wikilink" title="bicycle">bicycle</a>
<a href="factory" class="wikilink" title="factory">factory</a> in and
helped <a href="StrikeBike" class="wikilink"
title="takeover the bike factory">takeover the bike factory</a> and run
it as a <a href="Workers&#39;_Self-Management" class="wikilink"
title="self-managed workplace">self-managed workplace</a>. In 2009, FAU
members protested the Babylon Cinema in Berlin after the unjust firing
of workers, leading to the Berlin Court to forbid the FAU from calling
itself a union. Romanian construction workers with the FAU also began a
strike at the Mall of Berlin after they had not been paid for months.
They FAU also helps organise food delivery drivers, notably helping in
<a href="Black_Friday_(Germany)" class="wikilink"
title="Black Friday">Black Friday</a>.[^1]
